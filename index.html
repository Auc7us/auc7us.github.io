Keshav Sharan Pachipala

I am a PhD student in Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, working on embodied AI simulations at the Simulation-Based Engineering Lab (SBEL) under Prof. Dan Negrut.

I also serve as Lead ML Systems Engineer at Wisconsin Autonomous, where I work on real-time perception and autonomy for the Chevy Bolt. My research spans decentralized semantic collaboration for multi-agent search, high-fidelity autonomous vehicle testing, and embodied AI.

Previously, I was a Software Engineer at WCB Robotics Inc., leading autonomy on E.L.M.O.—the world's first ropeless facade-cleaning mobile robot. I also worked as an Embedded AI Intern at Asteria Aerospace, building real-time human MOT pipelines for surveillance UAVs.

Interests: Real-Time ML Systems, Embedded AI, 3D Perception, Embodied AI, VLMs, LLMs, NLP, Runtime Optimization, GenAI
